# Imogen’s Vision — Business Proposal Summary

## Overview

Imogen’s Vision is a developing health and wellbeing project focused on helping people make long-term, sustainable lifestyle changes. The proposal centres on practical support, motivation, behaviour change, and personalised guidance rather than quick fixes.

The initial context discussed was a clinic model supporting people with weight loss and wider health improvement. The service would combine professional clinical input with ongoing motivational support, helping patients build confidence, consistency, and healthier routines over time.

## Purpose of the Business

The purpose of Imogen’s Vision is to support people to improve their health and wellbeing in a realistic, sustainable way.

The business aims to:

- help people lose weight safely and sustainably
- support long-term lifestyle and habit change
- improve confidence and motivation
- provide regular check-ins and encouragement
- reduce reliance on medication where appropriate and clinically safe
- create a supportive, human, and non-judgemental experience

## Core Service Offer

The proposed service supports patients through a combination of:

- lifestyle and behaviour change support
- nutrition guidance
- regular motivational coaching
- personalised patient plans
- medical oversight where required
- medication or surgical pathways only where clinically appropriate

The emphasis is on long-term improvement, not short-term results.

## Patient Journey

Patients would first be assessed by appropriate professionals, such as a doctor and nutritionist. Depending on their needs, other specialists may also become involved.

Support is expected to be more frequent at the start and then reduce over time as the patient becomes more confident and independent.

Typical support may include:

- medical reviews monthly at first, then every 2–3 months
- nutrition support weekly at first, then less frequently
- motivational coaching weekly at first, adjusted based on patient need

The goal is to help patients build habits they can maintain outside of regular appointments.

## Team Model

The proposal describes a team-based approach where staff work closely together and regularly discuss patient progress.

Key team expectations include:

- attending regular team meetings
- discussing patient cases and concerns
- asking for help with complex situations
- supporting colleagues
- taking part in learning and development
- raising patient concerns quickly and professionally

The team is expected to be collaborative, supportive, and focused on improving patient outcomes.

## Imogen’s Role

Imogen’s proposed role is as a motivational coach.

Her role is not to provide medical advice, but to support patients emotionally and practically as they work towards healthier habits.

Key responsibilities include:

- helping patients stay motivated and consistent
- supporting patients when they struggle or lose confidence
- helping patients set realistic goals
- encouraging small, manageable behaviour changes
- checking in regularly with patients
- helping patients build long-term habits
- spotting concerns and raising them with the wider team
- contributing observations in team discussions
- supporting the overall patient experience

In simple terms, Imogen’s role is to help patients keep going, especially when change feels difficult.

## Individual Expectations for Imogen

As a team member, Imogen would be expected to:

- be reliable and professional
- build trust with patients
- communicate clearly and kindly
- be proactive in supporting patients
- ask for help when unsure
- report concerns or complaints quickly
- keep learning and improving her skills
- contribute positively to the team
- help promote the clinic where appropriate

Because this would be her first job, the role should include clear guidance, support, and opportunities to build confidence over time.

## 7 / 30 / 60 / 90 Day Development Plan

### First 7 Days — Getting Settled

Focus: understand the clinic, the patients, and how coaching fits in.

Key actions:

- meet the team
- learn how patients are supported
- observe coaching or patient sessions
- understand systems and processes
- ask questions and build confidence

### First 30 Days — Starting to Coach

Focus: begin supporting patients with guidance.

Key actions:

- support simple coaching conversations
- help patients set realistic goals
- attend team meetings
- build rapport with patients
- understand common challenges such as motivation, setbacks, and consistency

### First 60 Days — Working More Independently

Focus: take more ownership of coaching support.

Key actions:

- run coaching check-ins more independently
- help patients deal with setbacks
- flag concerns to the team
- contribute more actively in case discussions
- complete relevant learning or training

### First 90 Days — Making an Impact

Focus: become a trusted and proactive motivational coach.

Key actions:

- manage a group of patients confidently
- support long-term habit-building
- handle routine coaching challenges
- suggest improvements to the coaching approach
- contribute to patient experience and clinic growth

## Marketing and Growth Expectations

The proposal includes some expectation that staff help promote the clinic.

This may include:

- encouraging patient reviews
- writing short blog posts or website content
- sharing social media content
- supporting community awareness

This should be kept proportionate to the role, especially while Imogen is new and still learning.

## Work Setup

The role is expected to begin on a self-employed basis because the clinic is still developing and building its patient base.

The stated aim is to move towards employment contracts once the clinic has enough patients, potentially within around a year.

Important points:

- self-employed status means more personal responsibility
- employment later may include benefits such as holiday and sick pay
- the role is not expected to be exclusive
- Imogen may be able to do other work outside clinic hours

This area should be clarified carefully before accepting the role, including pay, hours, expectations, insurance, tax responsibilities, and employment rights.

## Key Strengths of the Proposal

- clear focus on sustainable health improvement
- supportive, patient-centred approach
- regular check-ins and ongoing motivation
- multidisciplinary team model
- opportunity for Imogen to grow into the role
- practical need for coaching alongside clinical care

## Key Risks or Questions

Important areas to clarify include:

- exact responsibilities for the motivational coach
- level of supervision and training provided
- boundaries between coaching and clinical advice
- pay and working hours
- self-employed terms
- safeguarding and complaints process
- how success will be measured
- expectations around marketing and content creation
- whether there is enough patient demand to support the role

## Simple Summary

Imogen’s Vision is a developing health and wellbeing proposal built around long-term lifestyle change, patient support, and motivational coaching. The business would help people improve their health through practical guidance, regular check-ins, and a supportive team approach.

Imogen’s role would be to support patients as a motivational coach, helping them stay consistent, build better habits, and keep going when they struggle. The role has strong potential, but the self-employed setup, supervision, pay, boundaries, and growth plan should be clearly agreed before moving forward.
